outputs/03_other_vlms/llama32_11b_vision/00_base/test/base_llama32_vision_test_243.csv

Under-disclosure vector logic:

Condition vector:
positive = true B/C
negative = true A

Purpose:
Detect whether the image-question pair is allowed to provide some location information.

Behavior vector:
positive = B_to_B, C_to_C
negative = B_to_A, C_to_A, C_to_B

Purpose:
Move generation away from under-disclosure and toward correct utility-preserving behavior.

Observation:
The under behavior vector is effective at reducing under-disclosure and improving correctness.
However, most useful thresholds increase over-disclosure by 2-3 cases.
The safe threshold 0.10 avoids increasing over-disclosure but gives a much smaller under-disclosure improvement.

Interpretation:
This matches the training table composition.
The under behavior vector mostly learns to convert C_to_B toward C_to_C.
That improves utility, but it can also push some A/B cases toward more specific disclosure and raise over-disclosure.
